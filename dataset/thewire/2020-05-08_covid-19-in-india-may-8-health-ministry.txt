Health Ministry Records Nearly 3,500 New COVID-19 Cases, Death Toll Jumps by 103
The government's latest update says India has 56,342 confirmed cases of COVID-19, of which 37,916 are active.
A health official scans the body temperature of a student who arrived in Srinagar, Thursday, May 7, 2020. Photo: PTI/S. Irfan
New Delhi: India continues to report around 3,000 fresh cases of the coronavirus infection every day. According to the health ministry’s website, the country has recorded 3,390 new cases in the past 24 hours and 103 fatalities were reported during this period.
The government’s latest update says India has 56,342 confirmed cases of COVID-19, of which 37,916 are active. There have been 1,886 deaths in the country due to the viral infection, while 16,539 people with the disease have been cured or discharged.
Maharashtra and Gujarat continue to report large number of deaths, with the former’s death tally increasing by 43 in the past 24 hours and by 29 in the latter. Maharashtra’s death toll now stands at 694, while Gujarat has reported 425 deaths. Madhya Pradesh (193) and West Bengal (151) have also recorded many deaths.
According to the health ministry’s data, the highest number of confirmed cases in the country is from Maharashtra at 17,974 followed by Gujarat at 7,012, Delhi at 5,980, Tamil Nadu at 5,409, Rajasthan at 3,427, Madhya Pradesh at 3,252 and Uttar Pradesh at 3,071.
The number of COVID-19 cases has gone up to 1,847 in Andhra Pradesh and 1,644 in Punjab.
The tally has risen to 1,548 in West Bengal, 1,123 in Telangana, 793 in Jammu and Kashmir, 705 in Karnataka, 625 in Haryana and 550 in Bihar.
Since May 2, the country has reported at least 2,400 new cases every day. On May 5, a day after the Centre allowed the lockdown to be relaxed in many areas, almost 3,900 new cases were identified, the highest single-day spike in the country yet.
If India continues to report around 3,000 new cases every day, the total number of cases in the country will surpass that of China – where the virus originated – in nine days. China has reported 83,975 cases of the viral infection so far and has been reporting a single-digit daily spike since the beginning of this month.
Peru, which has a total population of just 33 million, is also witnessing a rapid growth in cases of the viral infection. The country has reported 58,526 cases and stands just ahead of India.
The US accounts for almost a third of the global cases (3.84 million), reporting 1,257,718 cases of the viral infection so far. This is also nearly six times that of Spain, the country with the second highest number of infections. According to the Johns Hopkins University, COVID-19 has claimed the lives of 269,584 people so far.
