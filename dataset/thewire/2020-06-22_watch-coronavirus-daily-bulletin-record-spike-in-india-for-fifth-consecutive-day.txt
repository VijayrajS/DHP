Watch | Coronavirus Daily Bulletin: Record Spike in India for Fifth Consecutive Day
Kerala, Tamil Nadu and Odisha reported highest single-day spikes, while Mumbai recorded over 100 deaths for the second consecutive day.

More than 16,000 people contracted the virus in the past 24 hours, according to The Hindu. India has been reporting a record jump in the number of cases since the last four days. The total number of confirmed cases in India has breached 4 lakh and more than 13,000 people have succumbed to the virus. Of these, over 2 lakh people have recovered or have been discharged from hospitals.
However, according to the health ministry, India on Monday added 14,821 new COVID-19 cases pushing the infection tally to 4,25,282, while the death toll rose to 13,699 with 445 new fatalities.
Kerala, Tamil Nadu and Odisha reported highest single-day spikes. Mumbai recorded over 100 deaths for the second consecutive day. More than 3,561 people have succumbed to the virus so far.
