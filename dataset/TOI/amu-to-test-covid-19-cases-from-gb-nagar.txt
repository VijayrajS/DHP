Aligarh Muslim University to test Covid-19 cases from Gautam Buddh Nagar
2020-03-23
NOIDA: A backlog of pending tests of Covid-19 has prompted Gautam Buddh Nagar health department to send testing samples to Aligarh. 


Coronavirus in India live updates

The tests are being sent to the microbiology department of the Jawaharlal Nehru Medical College (JLMC) in Aligarh Muslim University (AMU) as the National Centre for Disease Control (NCDC) is piled up with pending test reports.

“We have decided to use JLMC lab facilities at AMU for the Covid-19 tests from GB Nagar. Since we have this additional facility nearby, the decision was taken to send collected samples to JLMC. We began sending the samples to the college from Friday (March 20) with around 100 samples. By Sunday, we had received 36 results out of which two tested positive from Greater Noida and 34 were negative,” said Dr Sunil Dohare, additional chief medical officer. Dohare added that the entire faculty of microbiology department is carrying out the lab test procedure.

“We don’t know how many members are there in a team but the entire faculty of the microbiology lab is engaged in the sample testing process,” he said. On Sunday, total travellers that were kept under surveillance were 1,657 out of whom 297 samples were collected, 8 were positive while 221 negative. According to sources, results of 69 people are awaited.

About 134 are hospitalised which include 7 positive at GIMS (one is a tour guide from Noida who is being treated in Delhi), 36 under observation at Dr Ambedkar SC/ST Hostel in Gautam Buddh University, Noida, 88 under observation in new district hospital building in Sector 39 and 3 at Child PGI hospital in Sector 30.
