Over 1,800 arrested in Uttarakhand for defying lockdown
2020-03-30
DEHRADUN: A total of 1,866 persons have been arrested across Uttarakhand for violating the lockdown imposed by the central government on March 24 to contain the spread of the coronavirus. 


 The Union government had announced a nationwide 21-day lockdown on Tuesday. In the following five days, Uttarakhand Police registered 329 FIRs under section 188 (disobedience to order duly promulgated by public servant) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC), seized 1,795 vehicles and challaned as many as 6,727 for running on roads despite the lockdown. In the process, police have collected a penalty fee to the tune of over Rs 29 lakh.

 Speaking to TOI, director general (DG) law and order Ashok Kumar, said, “Police are strictly enforcing the lockdown in the state and people violating it are being punished.”

 Notably, the state has been divided in 102 zones and 500 sectors for effective implementation of the lockdown order. Apart from that, 6,000 police personnel and additional 20 companies of Provincial Armed Constabulary (PAC) have been deployed in Uttarakhand.
