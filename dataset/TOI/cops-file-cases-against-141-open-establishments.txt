Covid-19: Pimpri Chinchwad police file cases against 141 open establishments
2020-03-20
PUNE: The Pimpri Chinchwad police on Thursday filed cases against 63 establishments, mostly eateries, shops and private workplaces, and their Pune counterparts registered 78 cases against business establishments for violating the district collector’s order banning assembly of five or more people at a place to arrest the spread of novel coronavirus.

All the 141 cases were filed under Section 188 of the IPC, which refers to disobedience of an order duly promulgated by a public servant, causing obstruction, annoyance or injury to the legal authority passing the order. It provides for a punishment of simple imprisonment for a month or fine up to Rs200.

Deputy commissioner of police Sudhir Hiremath, said, “Of the 63 establishments booked in Pimpri Chinchwad under the IPC Section 188, a total of 23 are from Chikhali, seven each are from Hinjewadi and Dehu Road, six each are from Bhosari MIDC and Nigdi, four are from Talegaon Dabhade, two each are from Bhosari, Alandi, Dighi and Ravet, and one each is from Shirgaon and Mhalunge.”

He said, “We shall ensure that shops, eateries and other establishments, which do not fall within the ambit of essential commodities, remain closed.”

Eight teams have been formed to shut the open establishments and file cases against them.

Hiremath said, “We have formed 20 teams, including officers from the Pimpri Chinchwad Municipal Corporation. These teams will visit residences of those home quarantined. Police will warn them of strict action if they are found roaming in their societies. The quarantined people must ensure that they stay at home. We have the list of home quarantined people.” 

In a meeting of various associations and senior corporators on Wednesday in Talegaon Dabhade, it was decided that all shops in the town would remain closed till March 21.
