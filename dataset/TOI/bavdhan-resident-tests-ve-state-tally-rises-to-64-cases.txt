Covid-19: Bavdhan resident tests positive, Maharashtra tally rises to 64 cases
2020-03-22
PUNE: A 25-year-old resident of Bavdhan tested positive for the novel coronavirus (Covid-19) infection on Saturday, taking the Covid-19 positive case tally in Pune district to 23.


The number of positive cases in the state rose to 64 on Saturday.

Of the 23 positive cases in Pune since March 9, a total of 11 patients are residents of the areas within the Pune Municipal Corporation (PMC) limits and the 12 others are residents of the Pimpri Chinchwad Municipal Corporation (PCMC). 

“The Bavdhan man hails from Ichalkanranji and was temporarily staying at his friend's place in Bavdhan after he returned from Ireland on Friday (March 20). He self-reported after he developed flu-like symptoms, following which he was isolated at Naidu Hospital the same day (March 20). His throat swab sample tested positive at the National Institute of Virology (NIV) on early Saturday (March 21) morning,” a civic health official said.

The man's close contacts, mainly his nine friends, have been quarantined at Naidu Hospital, and their throat swab samples have been sent to the NIV for testing. “Their reports are awaited,” an official said.
