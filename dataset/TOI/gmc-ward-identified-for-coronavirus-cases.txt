Goa Medical College ward identified for coronavirus cases
2020-01-28
PANAJI: The task force which was constituted for monitoring coronavirus cases, will hold its first meeting on Tuesday. 


On the instruction of health minister Vishwajit Rane, the task force was constituted to closely monitor all activities related to coronavirus. 

State epidemiologist Dr Utkarsh Betodkar said the Union ministry of health and family welfare held a meeting with all states on Monday over their preparedness to deal with if any cases of coronavirus. “The meeting was held to check our preparedness,” he said.

A ward at Goa Medical College (GMC) and hospital has been identified to keep coronavirus patients in isolation. Samples of suspected cases will be sent to the National Institute of Virology, Pune, for testing, he said. 

GMC dean Dr S M Bandekar held a review meeting on Monday, and issued a “call for vigilance” regarding the virus. 

Bandekar said while cases will be treated in the isolation ward that has been identified at GMC, the hospital pharmacy has enough stock of the required medicine.
