UP MPs, MLAs join hands, donate development funds for prevention against Covid-19
2020-03-25
LUCKNOW: Joining battle against coronavirus, scores of public representatives across Uttar Pradesh have offered to donate from their local area development funds to support preventive measures.


Some of them have specified that the fund could be used to support the poor and needy who have limited means to sustain themselves. Deputy CM Keshav Prasad Maurya and energy minister Shrikant Sharma pledged Rs 1 crore for preventive measures and procurement of gloves and sanitisers.

Ambedkarnagar MP Ritesh Pandey announced Rs 50 lakh to the district magistrate contingency fund while Mirzapur MP Anupriya Patel and Unnao MP Sakshi Maharaj committed Rs 25 lakh each for relief work. Minister of state for basic education Satish Chandra Dwivedi provided Rs 25 lakh to CM’s relief fund and donated a month’s salary. 

Parliamentarian from Basti Harish Dwivedi and MLA from Saiyadraja seat in Chandauli promised Rs 20 lakh each.

MLAs Gorakhnath Baba (Milkipur), Shailendra Yadav Lalai (Shahganj in Jaunpur), Sangeeta Yadav (Chauri Chaura in Gorakhpur), Chaudhary Amar Singh (Shohratgarh in Siddharthnagar), Ajay Kumar Lallu (Tamkuhi Raj in Kushinagar) and MLC from Amethi Deepak Singh donated Rs 10 lakh each to their districts.

UPPCS Association has urged members to donate Rs 1,000 each to CM relief fund.
