Man booked for escaping quarantine
2020-03-26
BHOPAL: A 35-year-old man, who had returned from Dubai three days ago, was booked for allegedly escaping quarrantine on Tuesday. Meanwhile, four cases were registered across the city for allegedly violating curfew orders. 

MP Nagar police said that a scooter-borne 35-year-old man, a resident of Kotwali area, was stopped at checking point at Board Office Square on Tuesday morning. During questioning, he told cops that he returned from Dubai three days ago. The screening team at airport told him to remain in quarantine for 14 days and posters of Covid-19 suspect were pasted outside his house. He said that he was feeling suffocated and so moved out. Police rushed him to a hospital and registered a case for violating quarrantine order. 

Nishantpura police registered cases were against five for opening shops and roaming out on Tuesday. Parwalia police also registered a case against a crusher unit owner for operating the plant.
