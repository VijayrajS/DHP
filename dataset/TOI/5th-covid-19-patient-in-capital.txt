Delhi: 500 contacts of all five coronavirus patients under surveillance
2020-03-12
NEW DELHI: The number of Covid-19 cases in Delhi rose to five on Wednesday, with a 46-year-old man with a history of travel to Japan, Geneva and Italy being confirmed positive for the novel coronavirus infection.



 
   Coronavirus outbreak live updates 
 
 

The man, a resident of Janakpuri in west Delhi, has been admitted to Ram Manohar Lohia (RML) Hospital, officials said. “There are nine members in his family, all are asymptomatic except his mother,” said an official.


He added that surveillance of nearby 50 houses has been done and authorities were keeping a close watch on anyone who may have come in contact with the patient.

Of the five cases confirmed in Delhi, the first one was a resident of Mayur Vihar in east Delhi. The rest are from West Delhi, sources said.

“Nearly 500 people who came in contact with the five persons found positive for Covid-19 are under surveillance. Some of them are from outside the state,” said a source. He added that 48 people suspected to be infected are admitted at Safdarjung and RML hospitals — the two nodal centers for treatment of Covid-19 cases in Delhi. “The state headquarters control room received 38 calls on Wednesday for Covid-19 related queries. More than 20 calls were made to CATS for ambulance services,” an official of the public health department said.
