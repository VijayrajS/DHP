U’khand to treat coronavirus as ‘notified disaster’
2020-03-16
Dehradun: The Uttarakhand government is going to treat coronavirus as a ‘notified disaster’ in order to contain the spread of the infectious virus. The notification in the regard was issued by the Union home ministry on Saturday.

Officials told TOI that the inclusion of coronavirus in the list of notified disasters will allow state government to utilise money from the State Disaster Response Force (SDRF) fund to pay ex-gratia payment to families of deceased and for the treatment of the patients suffering from the virus. 

“After the central government included Covid-19 in the list of notified disasters, the state will be able to provide assistance for the treatment and other purposes under SDRF allocation,” said Piyoosh Rautela, executive director of disaster mitigation and management centre (DMMC).

As per the notification issued by the Union home ministry, the families of the person(s) dying of the contagious virus, which includes those involved in relief operations or associated in response activities, will get an ex-gratia of Rs 4 lakh.

Also, the cost of hospitalisation for managing Covid-19 symptoms will also be borne by the state government. Moreover, the procurement of equipment to curb the virus and to strengthen the surveillance and control measures will be incurred from SDRF funds as well.

The state government had on Saturday specified that “a fund corpus of Rs 50 crore has been earmarked for creating isolation wards and other preparations for Covid-19.
