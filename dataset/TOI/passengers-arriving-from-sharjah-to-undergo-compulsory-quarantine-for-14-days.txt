Covid-19 in Surat: Passengers arriving from Sharjah to undergo compulsory quarantine for 14 days
2020-03-18
SURAT: Surat Municipal Corporation’s (SMC) health department has started preparations for compulsory quarantine of passengers arriving from Sharjah at Surat airport to check the spread of Covid-19 in the Diamond City on Tuesday.

The civic body has set up a 1,000-bed quarantine facility at Samras hostel of Veer Narmad South Gujarat University (VNSGU) where passengers from Sharjah will be kept. The decision was taken on the basis of travel advisory issued by central government’s health ministry for compulsory quarantine for a minimum period of 14 days for passengers arriving in the country from UAE, Oman, Qatar and Kuwait from March 18. 

Dr Ashish Naik, deputy municipal commissioner (health and hospital), told TOI, “We have created a 1,000-bed quarantine facility at VNSGU’s Samras hostel for compulsory quarantine of passengers arriving from Sharjah at Surat airport. They will be kept under observation for 14 days and this exercise for Sharjah passengers will go on till March 31.” 

Surat airport has a lone international flight between Surat and Sharjah operated by Air India Express. The flight is operated four days a week and an average of 100 passengers arrive in each flight. 

About 647 passengers have undergone thermal screening at the airport and not a single suspected coronavirus case has been detected since March 4. SMC has deputed medical staff of five people, including two doctors, for screening passengers arriving from Sharjah.
