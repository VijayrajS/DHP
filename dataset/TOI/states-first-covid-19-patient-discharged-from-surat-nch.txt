State’s first COVID-19 patient discharged from Surat NCH
2020-03-30
Surat: A 21-year-old woman, who tested positive for COVID-19, walked out of the New Civil Hospital on Monday. After two test reports of the woman came negative, health authorities were confident about her full recovery and discharged her.Dr Ashish Naik, deputy municipal commissioner (health and hospital), told TOI, “Her third sample also tested negative on Sunday and she has recovered fully after 14 days of care and management.”The woman, a resident of Parle Point, was admitted in the isolation ward of the NCH on March 16 soon after her arrival from London. She tested positive for COVID-19 after her samples were sent to National Institute of Virology (NIV) in Pune on March 19. A senior health officer said, “There is another COVID-19 positive patient in the NCH, admitted in the isolation ward on March 19, who is also on the path of recovery. He is responding well to the treatment and that his body temperature is well under control for the past few days. The other five COVID-19 patients are also stable.” Meanwhile, a 26-year-old male from city’s Udhana having travel history to UAE tested positive for COVID-19 on Sunday. The patient was admitted in the isolation ward of the NCH after he complained of severe cough, cold and fever. The SMC’s health department stated that about 12 new suspected cases were reported on Sunday. Majority of them have no travel history. Dr Naik said, “Cases of local transmission are on the rise in the city. There are patients with no travel history and others who have been just to Maharashtra or New Delhi.”
