Coronavirus scare: More companies move to work from home option
2020-03-23
PUNE: Many companies have moved to work-from-home totally or are actively considering working remotely. 


In recurring calls to industry representatives, government authorities have been telling companies to voluntarily comply with work-from-home arrangements. 

“Members sought a day or two to comply. They have offered to shut down temporarily,” Prashant Girbane, director general, MCCIA, said. 

Companies have decided to scale down operations. At least 4,000 small and micro supply industries associated with the Pimpri Chinchwad Small Scale Industries Association have said they will remain in shut-down mode till March 31. 

“We have decided to rapidly scale down activities at Pune site to get to skeletal operations by end of Monday (March 23) and be ready for plant closure by end of Tuesday (March 24) if the situation warrants,” Tata Motors said in a statement.

TOI wrote to and followed-up with a dozen IT companies who had allowed only partial remote working option till Wednesday evening. None of the companies responded with the exact numbers working from home, but said they continue to watch the situation and weigh their options.

Companies like HSBC, Credit Suisse, Infosys have now told their employees to work from home. Infosys told its employees that it will enable remote connections from home by shifting desktops to the homes of staffers.

Harshvendra Soin, chief people officer at Tech Mahindra, said, “ We are arranging machines, if required, for our associates to facilitate work from home. We are taking all necessary preventive measures through sanitation, social distancing, etc.”

Automaker Mahindra and Mahindra said, “ We have decided to suspend the manufacturing operations at our Nagpur plant with immediate effect and Chakan (Pune) and Kandivali (Mumbai) from Monday night onwards”.
