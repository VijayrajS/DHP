Maharashtra: Covid-19 testing to begin at GMCH
2020-03-23
AURANGABAD: The Government Medical College and Hospital (GMCH) will start conducting Covid-19 tests from the coming week, district civil surgeon Sundar Kulkarni said on Saturday.


Chief minister Uddhav Thackeray had sought additional testing centres for Covid-19 from the central government. Subsequently, GMCH will be getting a testing centre.

Currently, all swab samples for coronavirus tests are being sent to the National Institute of Virology (NIV) in Pune. However, due to increasing load of tests, demand was being made for increasing the coronavirus labs.

On March 15, public health minister Rajesh Tope had announced that coronavirus laboratories will soon be started at the government medical colleges in Dhule, Miraj, Solapur and Aurangabad.

On Saturday, Aurangabad district civil surgeon said the testing facility will be started at GMCH anytime this week.

GMCH is the only state-run tertiary care hospital in the district. The hospital caters to patients from nearby Jalna, Jalgaon, Ahmednagar, Parbhani, Beed, Osmanabad and Hingoli on a large scale.

So far, 43 patients who were check for coronavirus from Aurangabad have tested negative. According to the data released by district disaster management authority on Sunday, as many as 1,043 people have been quarantined at different institutions and at home across the district. This includes 205 people with travel history.

The Aurangabad Municipal Corporation (AMC) has screened 59,253 people at 6 of its screening centres until 2 pm on Sunday. Of these 56 people were found with symptoms like fever, cold and cough. “These people were referred to the GMCH and Chikalthana district civil hospital,” AMC said in a release.
