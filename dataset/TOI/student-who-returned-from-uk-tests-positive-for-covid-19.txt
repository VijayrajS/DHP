Student who returned from UK tests positive for Covid-19
2020-03-31
Agra: A 17-year-old student, who had returned from the United Kingdom on March 18, has tested positive for novel coronavirus (Covid-19) in Agra. The latest report has taken the total number of Covid-19 positive cases in the Taj city to 11.The UK returnee’s father is the owner of a group of private colleges. And the boy is a Class 11 student studying in Newcastle, UK. He along with his father visited the Agra district hospital on Saturday for tests. The samples were sent to King George’s Medical University (KGMU) and reports came out late on Sunday. His father has tested negative, so far.After his return from the UK, the student was asked to maintain self-quarantine for a few days, sources said. The health department has now placed the entire house under lockdown and sanitised the area. Contact mapping and screening of people in the neighbourhood has begun.Since March 2, as many as 11 Covid-19 cases have surfaced in Agra. Out of these, seven, including five members of a shoe exporter’s family, manager of the exporter’s factory and his wife, have been ‘cured’ and are currently quarantined at home. Three patients are being treated at the quarantine facility in SN Medical College.On Monday, district magistrate (DM) Prabhu N Singh said, “Seven patients have been cured. Samples of railways employee’s daughter, who had returned from Europe and had initially tested positive, following treatment at SN Medical College, has now tested negative, twice. She will be released soon.”“The health department is screening everyone who was in contact with the student. The neighbourhood is being sanitised. People are advised not to step out of their houses without any valid reason. They have been asked to call the helpline numbers ‘108’ and ‘102’ in case of any health issues,” the DM added.
