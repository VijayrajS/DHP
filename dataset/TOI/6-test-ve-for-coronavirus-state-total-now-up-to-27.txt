6 test positive for coronavirus, Telangana total now up to 27
2020-03-23
HYDERABAD: Six more persons in Telangana tested positive for coronavirus on Sunday. The total number of cases in the state has now risen to 27. All six persons had a travel history from foreign countries. The state health department said the persons have been isolated and are receiving treatment.


Among the six cases tested positive on Sunday, five are men and one a housewife from Hyderabad with a travel history to Dubai. Her husband and son have also tested positive and are in isolation centres. Authorities said several contacts including friends, family members and others were already quarantined as part of the containment process.

“Again, all these people are with travel history to London, Dubai, Scotland among others,” said chief minister K Chandrasekhar Rao. While two are from Andhra Pradesh, the remaining four are from Telangana. The two from AP include a 24-year-old from Guntur, who arrived from London via Dubai, while another student is a 26-year-old resident of Rajolu area in East Godavari district. He had returned from Sweden. 

Other male patients from Telangana include a 23-year-old student from Kukatpally, a 35-year-old from Manikonda who returned from Sweden and a 23-year-old student from Bhadradri Kothagudem who returned from London. Since March 19, the state government has stepped up its vigil and has placed thousands under quarantine.

Following a rise in coronavirus cases, the Centre on Sunday notified five districts of Telangana as lockdown districts. The districts include Bhadradri-Kothagudam, Hyderabad, Medchal, Rangareddy and Sangareddy. More cases of people with travel history have been reported from these areas. In the afternoon health minister, Eatala Rajender reviewed the situation in the state with health department officials.
