Bhopal: Covid-19 journalist booked for ‘endangering life of others’
2020-03-28
BHOPAL: Shyamla Hills police, acting on complaint filed by CMHO Dr Sudhir Dehariya, on Friday night registered a case against the journalist who tested positive for Covid-19 on March 25. 


According to ASP Manu Vyas, the complaint says that the scribe was instructed to stay at home after his daughter tested positive but he “roamed around in the city, attended press conferences and endangered the life of others”. 

He had attended a press conference called by former CM Kamal Nath. Now, all the journalists who attended the presser are in home quarantine. The scribe has been booked under IPC sections 188, 269 and 270.
